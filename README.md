# S255-System-Analysis-and-Design.


1. Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
DriverPass is an organization that assists learners in preparing to take their written and driving tests at the DMV. They needed to develop a cloud-based, web-based system that combined online classes, practice exams, and scheduling of driving lessons into one cohesive application. The system also had to provide multiple levels of access to users (customers, staff, and management), along with the ability to update users on the latest DMV rules and test information.

2. What did you do particularly well?
I produced effective visual representations of the System that display its functionality from multiple perspectives. I utilized Use Case, Activity, Sequence and Class Diagrams accurately depict user interaction with the system, the various types of interaction between users and system processes, as well as the overall structure of data used to support those processes within the system. This assisted me in making the overall design aspect of the System more straightforward to read and comprehend.

3. If you could choose one part of your work to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
My current diagrams would benefit from a more organized and precise presentation. I would look to limit the clutter present in several places and or the number of overlapping lines occurring on a single image. In addition to improving the layout of the diagrams, I would clarify and condense the explanations; therefore, making them easier for non-technical users who may have difficulty understanding what is being presented.

4. How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it important?
Using the business requirements, as well as by reviewing the interview data with users, I gathered what users needed in the system to be able to do things like schedule lessons, monitor their progress, and manage their accounts. I then incorporated those into the system by developing functionality for making reservations, defining user roles, and producing reports to help provide users with the information they needed to use the system effectively. By using an objective approach when planning the requirements of the system based upon the users' needs, I could ensure that the system would serve its intended purposes and be user-friendly.

5. How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future?
I begin by understanding the user needs and requirements and breaking the system down into discrete functioning units with the help of diagramming and modeling techniques. My focus is on ensuring that the overall system architecture is easy to read and scalable, while being both straightforward and representative of each subset. In subsequent systems I design, I will utilize similar approaches including UML diagramming, requirements analysis, and step-by-step system design to ensure consistency across multiple versions and that the system is fully utilized as intended and effectively meets user expectations.
